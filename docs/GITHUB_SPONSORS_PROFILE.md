# GitHub Sponsors Profile Copy
## Loptr Lab

---

## Profile Summary

Loptr Lab is building open prototype systems, contributor-ready documentation,
and accessibility-conscious engine work designed for long-term sustainability.

Your sponsorship helps fund infrastructure, maintenance, technical writing,
accessibility engineering, and recurring development time across the Loptr Lab
ecosystem — including Veiled Dominion.

Our goal is to move from one-time support to a predictable recurring structure
that keeps the work stable, legible, and open.

---

## Suggested Tier Copy

### $2 / month — Ashen Witness

Support the work and help keep Loptr Lab’s open prototype ecosystem alive.

This tier is for people who want to say “keep going.” It helps cover the quiet,
unseen maintenance behind open development: docs, cleanup, issue triage, and
infrastructure drift.

You may be listed as a community supporter if public recognition is enabled.

### $5 / month — Veiled Supporter

Help sustain recurring maintenance for Veiled Dominion and related Loptr Lab
open-source work.

This tier supports documentation, contributor onboarding, accessibility-first
engineering work, and long-term prototype stability.

If you want, your name or handle may be listed in sponsor recognition.

### $10 / month — Circle of Restraint

Support the deeper architectural and accessibility work that makes this project
different.

This tier helps fund recurring maintenance, safer rendering experimentation,
project structure, and the technical documentation that keeps the work legible
to contributors, reviewers, and future collaborators.

Includes optional public recognition as a sponsor.

### $25 / month — Working Contributor

For freelancers, indie developers, and technical practitioners who want to help
keep Loptr Lab’s open work stable, documented, and moving.

Your support helps fund predictable maintenance time for issue review,
documentation updates, and roadmap continuity.

This tier is meant for people who rely on healthy open ecosystems and want to
help keep one standing.

### $50 / month — Production Supporter

For professionals and small studios who benefit from clear maintenance,
documentation, and direct technical continuity.

This tier helps fund recurring upkeep for contributor review, issue triage,
accessibility engineering documentation, and release-path stability.

Sponsors at this level may be recognized in sponsor listings and future project
support pages if desired.

### $100 / month — Studio Ally

For small studios, consultants, and production-facing teams who want to support
serious open-source maintenance and accessibility-conscious engine development.

This tier helps create protected maintenance time for roadmap execution,
production-relevant issue review, and long-term sustainability planning.

If useful, this tier can also serve as a starting point for direct contact about
higher-touch sponsorship or invoicing arrangements.

### $250 / month — Infrastructure Patron

For organizations that want to help fund durable open infrastructure, technical
documentation, and long-horizon maintenance.

This tier supports recurring engineering time, contributor review capacity,
accessibility implementation planning, and the continuity required to keep an
open prototype ecosystem healthy.

Organization recognition can be included where appropriate and desired.

### $500 / month — Strategic Sponsor

For companies and production teams that receive meaningful value from active,
maintained open-source work.

This tier helps fund predictable core maintenance, higher-priority review
attention, accessibility-conscious technical implementation, and long-term
project continuity.

If needed, invoicing or procurement-compatible arrangements can be discussed
directly.

### $1,000 / month — Foundational Sponsor

For organizations that want to materially strengthen Loptr Lab’s long-term
independence and engineering capacity.

This tier helps support sustained core development time, strategic maintenance,
accessibility-first implementation work, and the long-term stability of the
open project ecosystem.

For organizations operating through standard procurement channels, direct
sponsorship arrangements can be coordinated by contact.
