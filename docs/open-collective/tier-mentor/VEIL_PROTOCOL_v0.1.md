DLM SOURCE CODE: SENSORY ARCHITECTURE FOR VERTICAL GEOMETRY

1.0 EXECUTIVE DIRECTIVE

Standard apparel treats tall bodies as an afterthought—simply adding length to a standard block. Standard apparel treats neurodivergent nervous systems as a marketing trend—slapping "sensory friendly" on a stiff, rustling polyester shirt.

Daddy’s Little Mortis does neither.

This source code outlines the manufacturing specifications for The Veil Protocol: A line of garments engineered specifically for vertically inclined individuals (6'0" - 7'0"+) requiring active nervous system regulation (proprioceptive input, deep pressure therapy, acoustic dampening) without sacrificing the DLM dark-arts aesthetic.

2.0 THE GEOMETRY (Sizing Matrix)

We do not use "standard plus." We use architectural scaling.

 Base Block: All patterns must be drafted from a 6'4" / 220lb athletic-tall baseline.
 Proportional Scaling:
 Torso length (HPS to waist): +2.0" over standard.
 Sleeve length (shoulder to cuff): +3.5" over standard.
 Inseam: +4.0" over standard (Base 36" inseam).
 Critical: Shoulder width must remain proportional. Tall bodies are not necessarily wide bodies; do not simply scale up the X-axis, which causes neckline droop and shoulder creeps.
3.0 THE NERVOUS SYSTEM (Material Specifications)

The fabric is the medicine. All textiles must pass the following sensory protocols:

3.1 Acoustic Dampening (Silent Weave)

 Requirement: Zero audible rustle. Stiff fabrics (nylon, taffeta, stiff canvas) are strictly prohibited.
 Spec: Must use low-friction micro-brushed knits, heavy-weight French terry, or dense double-weaves that absorb kinetic energy rather than reflecting it as sound.
3.2 Tactile Neutralization (Zero-Friction Interior)

 Requirement: No exposed interior seams.
 Spec: Bonded seams or ultra-soft flatlock stitching exclusively against the skin. All internal tags must be heat-pressed or laser-etched. No topstitching that leaves a ridge on the inside of the garment.
3.3 Deep Pressure Therapy (DPT) Integration

 Requirement: Garments must provide gentle, distributed weight without looking like medical "weighted clothing."
 Spec: Utilize hidden internal channels in the torso/shoulder panels (e.g., quilted lines) filled with premium micro-glass beads. Weight target: 10-15% of the wearer's body weight, distributed evenly across the shoulders and upper chest (the "Sanctuary" zone).
3.4 Thermo-Regulation

 Requirement: Nervous system dysregulation often causes rapid body temperature fluctuations.
 Spec: Base layers must feature phase-change material (PCM) yarns or highly breathable, moisture-wicking bamboo-charcoal blends.
4.0 THE AESTHETIC (DLM Material Translation)

Sensory gear looks medical. DLM looks like a void. You must merge them.

 "Void-Silk" (The Outer Shell): A heavy, ultra-matte, brushed jersey or woven that mimics the light-absorbing qualities of Musou Black. It must have a "dead" hand-feel (no sheen, no reflection), but drape elegantly over the tall frame to avoid the "tent" effect.
 "The Spark" (Interior Accents): Hidden interior panels (visible only when rolling up sleeves or lifting hoods) in a high-contrast, soft amber or blood-red. This provides a private sensory visual stim for the wearer without breaking the external gothic silhouette.
