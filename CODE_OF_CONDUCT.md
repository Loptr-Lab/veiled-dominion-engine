# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge
We as maintainers and contributors pledge to make participation in the Veiled Dominion community a respectful, constructive, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, experience level, or discipline.

We welcome contributors across engineering, design, technical writing, community operations, and related creative or systems-focused work.

## Our Standards
Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:
- Giving constructive, specific, and respectful feedback
- Assuming good intent while still communicating clearly about impact
- Welcoming contributors with different skill levels and backgrounds
- Crediting others for ideas, research, and implementation work
- Staying focused on the problem, ruleset, architecture, or workflow under discussion

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:
- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory, demeaning, or exclusionary language
- Trolling, dogpiling, or deliberately disruptive behavior
- Gatekeeping, dismissiveness, or hostility toward new contributors
- Spam, unsolicited promotion, or repeated off-topic self-advertising
- Sharing private information without permission

## Scope
This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, commits, project boards, email exchanges related to project collaboration, and community spaces operated for the project.

## Enforcement
Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to unacceptable behavior, including warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent removal from project participation spaces.

## Reporting
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to:
- questions@loptrlab.com for general community concerns
- security@loptrlab.com for security-sensitive matters

Please include relevant links, screenshots, or context where possible so maintainers can review promptly.
